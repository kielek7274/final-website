List of Top 20 Most Popular House Cats!

1. Ragdoll

The Ragdoll is a blue-eyed beauty, ranking No. 1 on CFA’s list of most popular cat breeds for four years in a row. This breed is easily recognized by their fluffy, long-haired coats and mellow disposition. They’re known to be affectionate, playful and intelligent. They’re also revered for their intelligence and friendly manner—and even get along with other animals and children. Ragdolls don’t shed as much as other cats due to their soft outer hair layer, but they will still need to be groomed during the change of seasons to keep their coat looking shiny and strong. Their weight varies from 10 to even 20 pounds, and their life expentancy eaches from 15 to even 25 years!



2. Maine Coon

Known as gentle giants, the Maine coon cat can weigh up to 20 pounds, making them the largest of pedigreed cats, according to CFA. This breed is a native American long-haired cat and is the official cat of Maine. They are known for being sturdy and rugged, with a shaggy coat that keeps them warm. Maine coon cats are friendly, great with kids and known for their incredible intelligence. They often like to play, especially in the water, and their paws are well-tufted to make walking in snow a breeze. Their coats only require occasional grooming to keep them silky smooth. Their weight varies between 9 to even 20 pounds, and their life expentancy is between 10 to even 15 years!

3. Devon Rex

With its oversized ears and large eyes, the Devon rex cat has a distinct look. They are a fun-loving breed with an attitude, who are known for being loyal and always by your side. Devon rex cats can be crafty, and tend to do well around other people and animals, including other cats, dogs, birds, ferrets and even rabbits. Their coats are unique and soft, with either short or wavy fur, and can easily be kept groomed by simply rubbing a warm washcloth through their fur. They are moderately active, so make sure to have lots of toys on hand. Their weight varies between 5 to 9 pounds, and they usually live between 14 to 17 years!

4. Exotic Shorthair

Squishy, soft, and cuddly, the exotic shorthair is like the teddy bear of cats. They are similar to their Persian cousins, but with a thicker, shorter coat that doesn’t require as much grooming. These kitties are a quiet breed, perfect for cat naps on your lap or playing easy games with their owner. Because of their short hair, you can get away with brushing them about once a week, making them ideal for busy families. The exotic shorthair is known for its round, flat face and a coat that comes in many different colors. Their weight varies between 8 to 15 pounds, and they live between 8 to 11 years!



5. Persian



The Persian cat, characterized by its distinctive flat face, large expressive eyes, and luxurious long coat, exudes an air of elegance and grace. With a calm and gentle demeanor, this breed is known for forming strong bonds with its owners. Regular grooming is essential to maintain the beauty of its coat, making it a delightful and affectionate companion.


Weight/Size 8-15 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 8-11 years
Coat and color White, blue, chocolate, lilac, black, silver, gold, tabby, and bicolor and more
Characteristics Round head, small ears, big eyes and a long, luxurious coat; they are quiet and easy-going


6. British shorthair


The British Shorthair cat is known for its round face, dense and plush coat, and large, round eyes. This breed has a sturdy and compact build, with a calm and easygoing temperament. With a sweet expression and a variety of coat colors and patterns, the British Shorthair is a charming and affectionate feline companion.


Weight/Size 8-16 Pounds
Height 11-14 Inches
Life Expentancy 12-20 years
Coat and color A blue-gray coat is popular, but a British shorthair can come in any color or pattern
Characteristics Textured coat, stocky build and calm demeanor


7. Abyssinian



The Abyssinian cat is a graceful and slender breed, adorned with a short, ticked coat that gives it a stunning wild appearance. Known for its vibrant and warm-colored fur, large ears, and expressive almond-shaped eyes, the Abyssinian radiates an aura of energy and curiosity. Highly intelligent and playful, these cats thrive on interaction and make affectionate companions with their lively and engaging personalities.


Weight/Size 6-12 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 12-14 years
Coat and color Ticked coat that can be cinnamon, chocolate, ruddy, blue, fawn, lilac or silver
Characteristics Muscular body, large ears, arched neck that resembles cats from ancient Egypt


8. Domestic non-pedigreed cats



Domestic cats, the most common and diverse of all feline companions, come in various breeds, colors, and personalities. Adaptable and often characterized by their independence, domestic cats can range from sleek and slender to fluffy and robust. Renowned for their agility, grooming habits, and varied vocalizations, these beloved pets have integrated themselves into homes worldwide, providing companionship and joy to their human counterparts.

9. Scottish fold



The Scottish Fold cat is recognized for its distinctive folded ears that give it an owl-like appearance. With a round face, large expressive eyes, and a plush coat, this breed exudes charm and sweetness. Known for their gentle and easygoing nature, Scottish Folds make affectionate companions, often forming strong bonds with their human families.


Weight/Size 7-10 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 11-14 years
Coat and color All colors and patterns, short and long hair
Characteristics Folded or straight ears with round eyes and a round head; sweet, loyal demeanor


10. Sphynx



The Sphynx cat is a unique and distinctive breed known for its lack of a coat, revealing a soft, warm, and nearly hairless body. Characterized by large ears, lemon-shaped eyes, and a wrinkled skin, the Sphynx has an inquisitive and extroverted personality. Despite their lack of fur, Sphynx cats are known for seeking warmth and are often described as affectionate, playful, and social pets.


Weight/Size 7-14 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 15+ years
Coat and color Hairless breed that comes in a variety of colors and patterns
Characteristics Large ears, hairless body, outgoing personality


11. Siberian



The Siberian cat is a robust and muscular breed known for its thick, water-resistant triple coat, which helps it withstand harsh climates. Originating from the Siberian taiga, these cats have large, round eyes and a bushy tail. With a friendly and outgoing nature, Siberians are often touted as hypoallergenic, making them a popular choice for those with cat allergies.


Weight/Size 17-26 Pounds
Height 9-11 Inches
Life Expentancy 11-18 years
Coat and color All colors and patterns on a medium-to-long hair triple coat
Characteristics Affectionate, playful and intelligent


12. American shorthair



The American Shorthair cat is a versatile and adaptable breed, known for its classic American appearance and wide range of coat colors and patterns. With a sturdy build, round face, and expressive eyes, American Shorthairs are friendly, easygoing, and make excellent family pets. Renowned for their longevity and robust health, these cats are cherished for their amiable temperament and low-maintenance grooming needs.


Weight/Size 6-15 Pounds
Height 10-14 Inches
Life Expentancy 15+ years
Coat and color Short, wiry coat in traditional colors, including a tabby
Characteristics Adaptable, independent, easygoing and friendly


13. Bengal



The Bengal cat is a striking and distinctive breed known for its distinctive spotted or marbled coat, reminiscent of a wild leopard. With a sleek and athletic build, large expressive eyes, and a short, dense coat with vibrant colors, the Bengal exudes an exotic and luxurious appearance. Renowned for their active and playful nature, Bengals are often described as intelligent, curious, and energetic feline companions.


Weight/Size 6-15 Pounds
Height 14-18 Inches
Life Expentancy 12-20 years
Coat and color Richly colored, highly contrasted coat of spots or marbling
Characteristics Active, playful, intelligent


14. Russian blue



The Russian Blue cat is a graceful and elegant breed known for its short, dense, and silvery-blue coat, which gives it a regal appearance. With striking green eyes, a wedge-shaped head, and a slim, muscular body, Russian Blues are often described as reserved yet affectionate. Renowned for their gentle demeanor and loyalty, these cats make loving companions and are recognized for their quiet nature and adaptability to various living environments.


Weight/Size 10-14 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 10-20+ years
Coat and color Short, blue and silver coat
Characteristics Intelligent, affectionate, talkative


15. Norwegian forest cat



The Norwegian Forest Cat is a large and majestic breed, known for its robust build, long tufted ears, and a luxurious, weather-resistant double coat. Originating from the forests of Norway, these cats have a bushy tail and tufted paws, resembling a wild, woodland creature. Norwegian Forest Cats are often described as friendly, intelligent, and agile, making them excellent hunters and cherished companions.


Weight/Size 8-18 Pounds
Height 9-12 Inches
Life Expentancy 13+ years
Coat and color Semi-long-haired waterproof double coat that comes in all colors
Characteristics A rugged-looking coat designed for cold Scandinavian winters, almond-shaped eyes, calm demeanor


16. Oriental shorthair



The Oriental Shorthair is a sleek and elegant cat breed, recognized for its slender body, large ears, and almond-shaped eyes. Available in a variety of coat colors and patterns, the breed is an extension of the Siamese family. Renowned for their vocal and social nature, Oriental Shorthairs are affectionate, active, and enjoy engaging with their human companions, making them delightful and interactive pets.


Weight/Size 5-12 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 10-20 years
Coat and color Short coat in a wide array of colors including traditional solid, tabby, tortie and torbie
Characteristics Large ears, almond-shaped eyes, long legs, elegant stature


17. Siamese



The Siamese cat is a sleek and vocal breed with striking blue almond-shaped eyes and distinct color points. Known for their intelligence and playfulness, Siamese cats are highly social, forming strong bonds with their owners and thriving in interactive environments. Their slender build, large ears, and affectionate nature make them a popular and engaging feline companion.


Weight/Size 5-12 Pounds
Height 8-10 Inches
Life Expentancy 10-20 years
Coat and color Light colored coat with darker extremities and facial area known as “seal point” pattern; points can also come in chocolate, blue, lilac, cinnamon, fawn, red and cream
Characteristics Medium-sized, shorthair cat with a distinct coat coloring and deep blue eyes; social and intelligent


18. Cornish rex



The Cornish Rex cat is a distinctive breed known for its curly, short coat, large ears, and slender body. With a unique and soft fur texture, they lack the typical guard hairs, resulting in a coat that feels remarkably warm and soft to the touch. Cornish Rex cats are often described as playful, affectionate, and highly social, making them engaging and charming companions.


Weight/Size 6-9 Pounds
Height 12-14 Inches
Life Expentancy 9-13 years
Coat and color Soft, curly coat that comes in many different colors
Characteristics Egg-shaped head, expressive ears, curly coat, friendly personality


19. Selkirk rex



The Selkirk Rex cat is recognized for its distinct and curly coat, characterized by soft, plush curls covering its body. Unlike other curly-coated breeds, Selkirk Rex has a more robust build, round face, and large, expressive eyes. Known for their affectionate and gentle nature, Selkirk Rex cats make delightful companions, adding a touch of uniqueness with their curly coat and friendly demeanor.


Weight/Size 9-11 Pounds
Height 7-12 Inches
Life Expentancy 10-15 years
Coat and color Short- and long-haired curly coat in many different colors
Characteristics Extremely curly coat, easy-going and relaxed


20. Birman



The Birman cat is an elegant breed known for its striking appearance, characterized by a silky, color-pointed coat, large blue almond-shaped eyes, and contrasting white "gloves" on its paws. With a strong and muscular build, Birmans have a distinctive Roman nose and a sweet, gentle expression. Renowned for their affectionate and sociable nature, Birmans make wonderful companions, often forming strong bonds with their human families.


Weight/Size 6-14 Pounds
Height 8-11 Inches
Life Expentancy 9-15 years
Coat and color Born white, then turns seal, blue, lilac, chocolate, red, cream and tortie with white paws
Characteristics Striking blue eyes, white paws and a gentle demeanor
